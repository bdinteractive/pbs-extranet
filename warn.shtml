PBS WARN - Public Safety Reliability Through Broadcast Technology

  • Jun 12, 2015
  • 0 Comments
  • Share
  • Compare Updated with Original

PBS WARN is a technology and grant program that that allows PTV stations to easily expand their public safety capabilities - and meet federal obligations - through the free, over-the-air delivery of Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA).

In 2010, the FCC mandated that public television stations provide a redundant diverse path for WEA, ensuring that cellular carriers receive the alerts, even in the event that the primary system does not work as intended. PBS WARN combines the geo-targeting and immediacy of cellular telephony with the resilience and durability of broadcast television.

Funded by the United States Department of Commerce, PBS WARN provided stations with all of the necessary equipment for the delivery of these WEA messages and also provided sub-grants for power back-up equipment to ensure the alerts are transmitted, even during power outages.

Emergency Alerts Get Mobile Through WEA

Modern cell phones are equipped with technology to provide a wide variety of local and national emergency notifications; however, even the most robust cellular network can be pushed past its limits by the huge influx of messages generated by large-scale emergencies. The Wireless Emergency Alert system enables cellular carriers to bypass network congestion so that these critically important messages can get to the people who most need to see them.

The PBS WARN redundant message delivery system provides further reliability through the intersection of broadcast transmission and cellular mobile delivery. Once the equipment is in place, the PBS WARN system will pass the alerts without requiring additional effort from the station or the carriers.

A New Public Safety Resource

WEA is a national public safety system that informs communities of Amber Alerts, imminent threats to safety or life, and Presidential Alerts via geographically-targeted, 90-character text-like messages from authorized government alerting authorities to enabled mobile devices.

Normal WEA PathWEA messages are designed to complement the Emergency Alerting System (EAS) and are drawn from the same IPAWS-OPEN database used by FEMA to power EAS, NOAA Weather Radio and even emergency street signs -- adding a new level of capabilities to current public safety messaging.

Public Service and Public Safety

The PBS WARN program is truly public service broadcasting. PBS WARN is part of a national partnership with the shared goal of public safety. The following organizations have been instrumental in establishing the PBS WARN system:

  • The Association of Public Television Stations
  • Commercial Mobile Service Providers
  • Department of Homeland Security
  • Federal Communications Commission
  • Federal Emergency Management Agency
  • United States Department of Commerce

WEAs Save Lives

On July 1, 2013, drivers in East Windsor, Connecticut saw an immense white object fly across the sky and slam into the ground. They had just witnessed a tornado uproot a municipal sports dome housing a summer camp with 29 kids and five counselors.

The camp director's WEA-capable cellular phone commanded her attention through distinct vibrations and alert tones. She saw the Wireless Emergency Alert for the tornado and moved everyone to safety. Two minutes later, the dome was a crumpled mass on the side of the highway.

If the tornado had struck prior to the inception of the Wireless Emergency Alert system (WEA), the counselor would not have received warning of an approaching tornado, and those 34 people might not have escaped the dome.

PBS WARN allows local stations to be a partner in an essential public safety system that saves lives in communities across the country.
